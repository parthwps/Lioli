What is the Difference Between Porcelain Tiles and Ceramic Tiles

Although porcelain tiles and ceramic tiles share many similarities, a quick glance at the installed products can sometimes be difficult to tell them apart.

Both the tiles are clay-based and kiln-fired, but there are few differences in ingredients and production methods and porcelain is technically a specialised type of ceramic. This means one might be a little more advantageous for your project.

Depending on how you intend to use the space, you may want to consider porcelain vs. ceramic tiles, which is better for your needs! Here, you will get to know the difference and their best uses.

Porcelain Tiles

Porcelain tiles are made of refined clay, with ultrafine sand and feldspar added to the mixture. After being kiln-fired at high temperatures and transformed, they look like stone, wood, concrete or materials. This clay-based construction makes porcelain tiles a subtype of ceramic tiles. However, porcelain tile is harder to construct, durable and long-lasting than non-porcelain ceramic tiles. Ceramic Tiles

Ceramic tiles are made with specific clay (with red, brown and white clay) and kiln-fired at a higher temperature but lower than porcelain tiles to reduce water level content, making them less dense, softer and more porous. The clay used in it is less refined hence it is less durable than porcelain tile. Ceramic tile is more affordable with numerous design options.

Comparison Between Porcelain Tiles VS. Ceramic Tiles:

Water and stain resistance capacity

Porcelain tiles

Porcelain tiles are well crafted, denser and less porous than ceramic tiles. It is categorised as impervious tiles which means the water absorption rate is less than 0.5 % making them perfect for bathrooms and other damp areas. A low water absorption rate enables a tile to absorb the stain of food and beverages in a lesser percentage.

Ceramic tiles

The main difference between porcelain vs ceramic tile is water resistance. Ceramic tiles are more porous and it has a higher water absorption rate. With proper installation and good quality grout, it can be used in the bathroom and outside applications.

Durability

Porcelain tiles

Since porcelain tile has a highly dense material, it is much harder and can withstand heavy use over time than ceramic tile. Porcelain tile is more durable and less prone to wear. Which makes it ideal for both home and commercial use.

Ceramic tiles

Ceramic tiles are not as durable as porcelain tiles. Since it has a less dense material, it is also less hard and can be more versatile. It is better to avoid using at a higher traffic place. Our ceramic tile manufacturing company in India offers tiles that have a more natural feel.

Ceramic tiles

Ceramic tiles are less dense and durable compared to porcelain tiles. It is also easy to cut manually with a snap tile cutter. Ceramic tiles are easy to install since it is a softer material.

Prevents Growth of Germs and Moulds

Porcelain tiles

Porcelain tiles will remain the same with regular cleaning and do not harbour bacteria like some porous materials, and they are easy to keep clean over time. Due to the impervious material porcelain tiles prevent growth of germs and moulds.

Porcelain tiles are unglazed and have the same colour underneath while ceramic tiles often have a glazed surface and reveal different colours underneath.

Porcelain tiles' water absorption level is less than 0.5 % whereas ceramic tilesâ€™ water absorption level is more than 0.5%.
